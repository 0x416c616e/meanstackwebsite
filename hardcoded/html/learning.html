What is a Tariff? Are They Bad for Stocks?

A tariff is simply a tax on a good made by a foreign country and being exported to the U.S.

Stephen Smart, 3/6/2018

Conventional economic theory says that tariffs are bad because they raise prices for consumers more than they “save” jobs of competing domestic firms. Without a lot of evidence, economists have traditionally put some of the “blame” for the Great Depression on the infamous Smoot-Hawley Tariff of 1930. This view has become more nuanced over the decades, with Nobel laureate Paul Krugman saying only that “Where protectionism really mattered was in preventing a recovery in trade when production recovered.” Since the U.S. was indeed already mired in depression when the bill was passed, and since there is an inevitable lag period in the cumulative effect of the tariff, this may be more realistic than assigning a lot of the “blame” for the Depression on Smoot-Hawley.
