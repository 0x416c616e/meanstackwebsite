Can anyone predict recessions? Does the market pay attention?

The short answer to both questions is, no.

Stephen Smart, 3/21/2018

In general, no one can reliably predict the next recession with any degree of accuracy (let’s say to within a year, let alone to within shorter time intervals). We have the “exception that proves the rule”; Nouriel Roubini disputed government (and Larry Kudlow’s) claims that the fallout from the subprime (and later, prime) lending would be “contained” as then-Treasury Secretary Hank Paulson assured. But as prescient as Roubini was proved to be, especially after Bear Stearns went under in March 2008 (the most visible indicator of the mortgage collapse), he has not proved to be especially prescient since the recovery got underway in 2009.

Volatility Kills

High volatility kills investors – certainly financially, and occasionally literally.

Stephen Smart, 2/6/2018

Unhinged by large down moves and demoralized by the quick evaporation of the occasional big “relief rally” day, investors become irrational. They stay up at night plotting strategies which they completely reverse the next day. They obsessively follow every twitch of the market and castigate those who told them to “Stay the course.” Then, with their financial and personal life in tatters, they sell out at the bottom (of course no one knows it’s the bottom at the time).
